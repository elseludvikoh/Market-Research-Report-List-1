<p><p>Compacted Graphite Iron (CGI) is a type of cast iron characterized by its unique microstructure, which combines the properties of both graphite and metal. The market for CGI can be categorized into two main types based on the form of graphite present: spheroids and nodules. Spheroidal CGI features graphite particles that provide excellent mechanical properties and high strength, making it ideal for automotive and heavy machinery applications. Nodule CGI, on the other hand, is known for its enhanced ductility and toughness, suitable for a broader range of industrial applications.</p></p>
