<p><ul><li>Natural Mullite</li><li>Fused Mullite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The mullite market is primarily divided into two types: natural mullite and fused mullite. Natural mullite is derived from the alteration of aluminosilicate minerals in geological formations, offering unique properties valued in applications such as ceramics and refractories. In contrast, fused mullite is manufactured by chemically bonding alumina and silica at high temperatures, resulting in a uniform and high-performance material. Both types serve critical roles in industries like metallurgy and ceramics, catering to specific performance requirements.</p></p>
